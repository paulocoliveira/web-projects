200 years of History

At a point that advanced over the sea, later known as Ponta do Calabouço, between the beaches of Piaçaba and Santa Luzia, in the historic center of Rio de Janeiro, the Portuguese built in 1603 the Fortaleza de Santiago, the origin of the architectural complex that now houses the National Historical Museum.
